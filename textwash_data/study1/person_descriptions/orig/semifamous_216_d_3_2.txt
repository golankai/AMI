William P Bettendorf was a German-American inventor from the late 1800s-early 1900s. His most well known inventions includethe power lift sulky plow, the Bettendorf metal wheel and the one piece railroad truck frame. He founded the Bettendorf Axle Company along with his younger brother, Joseph and by 1910 the company grew to a workforce of 800. Whilst the company started as an agricultural implement manufacturer it quickly moved to manufacturing items for railroads. The company expanded in the early 19th century to include manufacturing, among other things, oil burners, toys and water pumps. Bettendorf had two children by his first wife, becoming a widow in 1901 before remarrying in 1908.
