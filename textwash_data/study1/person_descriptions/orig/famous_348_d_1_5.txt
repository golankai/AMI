Mick Jagger was born in England in 1943. He has been married once and had relationships with a number of other women. He has eight children. He is famous for his role as lead singer in the band The Rolling Stones. He is also a founding member of the band and writes songs. One of the band’s most notable songs is, ‘I Can’t Get No Satisfaction.’ Mick is well recognised for his moves and charisma on stage. He received a knighthood in 2003. This was controversial as some people believed he did not deserve this recognition. In 2019, he had heart surgery but was soon back on stage entertaining his fans.
