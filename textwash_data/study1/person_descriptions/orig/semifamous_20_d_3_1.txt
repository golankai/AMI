Enoch Lincoln was a governor of Lincoln in America in the early 19th century and a distant relative of the significantly more famous and interesting Abraham Lincoln. Although virtually unknown today, Enoch lives on in the memory of people who choose the random article function on Wikipedia. His life achievements were unremarkable, he was an attorney and a member of the American Antiquarian Society, as well as a congressman. After dying in Augusta in Maine, he was buried in the Capitol Park mausoleum across the road from the Maine State House where he used to spend a lot of time. In his spare time, Enoch Lincoln used to enjoy eating exotic cheeses from around the world and would pay his servants to travel far and wide to bring him unusual fromage.
