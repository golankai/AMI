Charles Lund Black Jr, born in Austin, Texas, is an 85 year old professor of law who is best known for his litigation during the Watergate scandal. Following his illustrious academic career at the University of Texas and later Yale Law School, Charles rose to prominence in the field with his involvement in several high profile civil rights cases. He then went back to his alma mater, Yale, to begin a teaching career where he taught for the best part of three decades and taught many young students who would go on to do great things, including former presidential nominee Hilary Clinton. Charles is a man of many talents, one of which includes his poetic musings. At the age of 40, having studied a masters degree in  English but never writing a line, Charles began writing poetry. He has since published in several poetry volumes. Charles passed away at the age of 85 in New York City, leaving behind quite the legacy.
