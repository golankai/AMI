Professor Charles Black was born on the 22nd of September 1915 and died on May the 5th 2001 aged 85. He was an American scholar of constitutional law, he taught as professor of law from 1947 to 1999. He also wrote poetry in his early 40's and produed three volumes, Telescopes and Islands, Owls Bay in Babylon and The Waking Passenger. He had an interest in race and civil rights inspired by a performance by Louis Armstrong at the Driskill Hotel in Austin. He was an outspoken critic of the death penalty. Professor Black authored Capital Punishment: The Inevitability of Caprice and Mistake, published in 1981.
