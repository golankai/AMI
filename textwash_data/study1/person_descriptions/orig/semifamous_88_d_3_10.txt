Irvin Woodberry ""Chester"" Brooks was born June 5, 1891 and died February 4, 1966. He was an American baseball player in the Negro leagues. He was born in Key West, Florida where he grew up with his mother and 5 brothers.  He always wanted to be a baseball player and loved playing little league as a child. 

During his playing career he was known as ""Irvin"". He was also nicknamed Chester, a reference to West Indian origins From 1918 to 1933 he played pitcher, infielder, and outfielder.

Brooks spent his entire playing career with the Brooklyn Royal Giants and was an outstanding hitter. Cumberland Posey, the influential owner of the Homestead Grays, named Brooks to his all-time Negro league baseball all-star team.
