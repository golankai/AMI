William Bettendorf was born in Illinois in the USA, in 1857. His father was German. He started out his working career as a messenger boy. He had jobs as a clerk in a hardware store and a machinistâ€™s apprentice before he went on to develop his own ideas. William was known for his inventions, which included the power lift sulky plow, the Bettendorf metal wheel and the one-piece railroad truck frame. With his brother, Joseph, he founded the Bettendorf Axle Company. He was married twice and had two children. His children and first wife all predeceased him. He was only 52 when he died in 1910. He is buried in Oakdale Cemetery in Davenport.
