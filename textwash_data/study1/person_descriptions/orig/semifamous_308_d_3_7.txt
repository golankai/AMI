Charles Black (full name Charles Lund Black Jr) was born on the 22nd of September 1915. He was an american scholar of constitutional law. He also taught law as a professor from 1947 to 1999. 
Charles Black was born in Austin in Texas. He graduated in 1935 from the University of Texas in Austin and he later got a Master's degree in English.
Charles Black was very against the death penalty and wrote a book called Capital Punishment: The Inevitability of Caprice and Mistake.
Mr Black is best known for his involvement in the well known Brown V Board of Education Supreme court Case which helped to end racial segregation in public schools.
Charles Black died on the 5th of May 2001 in New York City ages 81.
