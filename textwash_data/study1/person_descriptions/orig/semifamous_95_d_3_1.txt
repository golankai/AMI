Enoch Lincoln is a former Govenor of Maine who shares ancestory with former President of the United States of America, Abraham Lincoln. He was in office from 1827 until his death in 1829. He was born in Worcester, Massachusetts in 1807 and is an alumni of Harvard.

After passing the Bar exam, he worked as a District Attorney between the years of 1815 and 1818 after which he moved to Paris, Maine and continued to practise law.

In his role of Governor of Maine he was preceded by Albion Parris and
succeeded by	James W. Ripley.

Lincoln was also a member of the American Antiquarian Society with which now some of his personal papers and manuscripts now reside.
The town of Licoln in Maine is named after him.
