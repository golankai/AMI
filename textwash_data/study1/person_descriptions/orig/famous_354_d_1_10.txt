Sam Smith is a London born singer-songwriter. Winner of four grammys for their debut album 'In the Lonely Hour' which was release in 2014. Sam Smith was born in May 1992 and first hit the charts at the age of 20 on the Disclosure song 'Latch'. Sam Smith's first single was released not long after. 

Sam Smith came out as gay in the same month as his debut album was released and revealed that though having written about love in many of their song they've never actually experienced this themselves and have never been in a relationship before.

Smith has since had a duet with John Legend, had a number one hit in the UK charts and co-written and sung the single for 'writings on the wall' for the James Bond movie Spectre.

Sam shares their name with the famous American painter as well as a number of sports stars and an English actor.
