Charles Lund Black was born in September 1915, during the First World War, and died in May, 2001. He was born in Austin, Texas, which is where he went to university. He obtained a law degree from Yale School of Law, a prestigious institution. During the Second World War, he served in the Army Air Corps as a teacher. He was a great opponent of the death penalty, and worked on many civil rights cases. In 1956 he joined Yale Law School as a professor, and spent over thirty years of his career there. Charles starting poetry in his forties, eventually publishing three books, as well as various legal titles. He loved jazz music, and was especially fond of Louis Armstrong's works. He was married to Barbara Aronstein Black, who was herself a noted lawyer; she was Dean at Columbia Law School.
