Dr Ratke is best known for his work at the British museum. He has a particular interest in the history of Egypt and has been instrumental in bringing many treasured artefacts to the museum. Originally from Uganda he came to the UK as a teenager to study at Cambridge university, where he ga8ned a first class degree in Egyptology and then went on to study for a masters degree and his PhD. While at Cambridge he was a SLO a member of the famous footlights society which has been a breeding ground for comics such as French and Saunders, Monty Python and many others. He is regularly seen on television giving his expert opinion on all things related to the history of ancient Egypt. He is also the only person to have ever been on a winning University Challenge team in 5 consecutive years.
