Irvin Brooks was born in 1981, an era where the colour of your skin was a reason to get upset about. Irvin lived in Key West, Florida in the USA. Irving had to overcome many challenges at the time, however, it didn't stop him from becoming one of the best American baseball players, as named by Cumberland Posey, the owner of the Homestead Grays. Irvin played for the Brooklyn Royal Giants from 1918-1924 and also played for the Atlantic City Bacharach Giants in 1931. He spent his whole career playing for the Brooklyn royal giants.  He gained the name ""Chester"" from his West Indian origins by Cumberland in articles. He died in 1966 in New York.
