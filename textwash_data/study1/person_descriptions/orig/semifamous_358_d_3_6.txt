Malcolm used to be a cricketer for the Goliath team and did very well in his career.
Malcolm is not retired but loves to be around his family and friends. He was born in 1950, is a right handed and is a right handed bowler. This is what he was known best for in his profession.
He made his debut in the south of England in places like Devon and Cornwall. 
His highest run number for a game was 33 and his average was around 10. He predominantly worked in Gloucestershire in his career but did quit at some time and continued to represent the area in cricket. 
He represented the county in 112 championships his last representative game was in Dorset and he completed these games in the late ‘80’s namely from 1984 to 1988.
