Malcolm Dunstan is a 70 year former cricketer for England. He made his debut in 1969 was in the Minor Counties Championship for Cornwall. He made his first list A cricket was also playing for Cornwall against Glamorgan. 

Dunstan continued to play List A cricket even after leaving Gloucestershire, playing a further four List A matches for Cornwall. His final match was the 1986 Natwest Trophy against Derbyshire.

Over the years he played for Gloucestershire and Cornwall in various matches and tournaments notably captaining for Cornwall on occasion. 

In his 12 1st class matches, he  had a batting average of 16.64 over 283 runs, with a single half century high score of 52.

Over his career Malcolm Dunstan played 23 List A matches scoring an average of 10.80 over 216 runs
