Walter K. Chorn was a collegiate football play from Missouri, in the mid-west of America.  He attended Vanderbilt where he was in the Kappa Sigma fraternity. Throughout his short lived live, he worked as a Lawyer, football player in the Guard position and briefly an insurance superintendent.  He practiced law for 2 years in his own practice, based in Fayette, Missouri, before becoming the chief clerk in 1909 for the commission that revised statues.  He was both a guard and tackle for Dan McGugin's Vanderbilt Commodores football teams.  In 1906 he was selected to the All-Southern team and in 1912 he was selected for the second-team on an all-time Vanderbilt football team.  He died at the early age of 48 in Kansas City, Missouri.
