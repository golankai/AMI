Malcolm Stephen Thomas Dunstan (born 14 October 1950)the world famous cricketer with a batting average of 16.64 he was county champion for a record (his own record) three years 1971 to 1974 his record score and only ever half century came against Warwickshire in a county championship match where he scored a whopping 52 runs.
Sadly Malcom was never good enough to play for Yorkshire but he did play for many southern teams and represented Cornwall at county level and played in eight trophy matches the last of which was against the formidably average Dorset
and also played against Derbyshire in the 1986 Natwest trophy.
When nobody else was available he would captain his beloved and beleaguered Cornwall
