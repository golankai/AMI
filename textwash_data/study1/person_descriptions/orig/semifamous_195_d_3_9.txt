Walter K. Corn was born in Missouri on the 21st January 1885. He is best known for being a college football player for Vanderbilt Commodores football teams where he played in the positions guard and tackle. His career highlights were SIAA championship (1906)
All-Southern (1906) 1912 All-time Vandy second team. After his career finished opened a law office where he practiced for two years and then became chief clerk of the commission that revised the missouri statutes. He had a fruitful career in law where he became chief clerk of the supreme court  of missouri in 1913. Walter K. Corn passed away aged 48 in Missouri in unknown circumstances. 
It is unknown whether Walter married or had children.
