Mick Jagger is the singer of the band Rolling Stones. He was born on July 26, 1943, in Dartford, England. He developed an interest in American blues and R&B music at an early age and got his first guitar at 14. As a teenager, Jagger collected blues records from singer's such as Muddy Waters and Howlin' Wolf. When Jagger was old enough he was accepted to the London School of Economics in 1960. This is where the Rolling Stones was formed. During their early career the band mostly recorded cover versions of other people's songs, however Rolling Stones made the British charts in 1964 with a cover version of Bobby Womack's ""It's All Over Now.""
