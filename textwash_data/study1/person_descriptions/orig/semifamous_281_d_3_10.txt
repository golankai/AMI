Irvin Brooks was an American baseball player in the early 1900s. Born on June 5th 1891 in Key West, Florida. He played in the  'Negro Leagues' which was league built on professional African American baseball players. Irvin Brooks was apart of the Brooklyn Royal Giants he played as a pitcher, infielder and outfielder. He played from 1918 to 1933 and was seen as an influential star player his team was seen as the best 'all-star baseball team in the league'. Throughout his career, he was also given nicknames such as Chester as it was referenced in prints and articles by Cumberland Posey. He sadly passed away on February 4th 1966.
