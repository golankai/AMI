Walter Knaus Chorn was born on January 21 1885 and died on February 26 1933. He was a college football player, lawyer, and one time insurance superintendent of Missouri America. Walter attended  Central College in Fayette, Missouri and Vanderbilt University in Nashville, Tennessee.  He raduated from the Vanderbilt University with an LL.B. Chorn was a guard and tackle for Dan McGugin's Vanderbilt Commodores football teams, selected All-Southern in 1906, and second-team on an all-time Vanderbilt football team selected in 1912. He opened a law office in Fayette, Missouri after graduating. He practised for two years. In 1909 he became chief clerk of the commission that revised the Missouri statutes. In 1910 and 1911 he served in the state auditor's office, and in 1913 was chief clerk of the supreme court of Missouri.
