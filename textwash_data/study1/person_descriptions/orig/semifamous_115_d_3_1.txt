Enoch Lincoln, very distantly related to Abraham Lincoln, was born in Worcester, Massachusetts in 1827. His father, Levi Lincoln Sr., was a lawyer and most notably served as Attorney General under Thomas Jefferson. Enoch Lincoln attended Harvard University where he studied law. He was the district attorney of Salem, however, in 1818 left practicing law and began a successful career in politics. He moved to Maine and was elected the as a Democratic Republican representing Maine. He later served as the Governor on Maine from 1827 until his death in 1829. He was elected a member of the American Antiquarian Society, now the oldest historical society in America. The town Lincoln in Maine is named after him.
