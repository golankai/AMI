Walter Chorn grew up in a very small town in Missouri in 1885. Growing up he was a very keen football player, often playing with his brothers in the back yard. After doing well at school he studied Law at Vanderbilt University. 

At university he played for the home team and was selected for the All- Southern in 1909, a team made up of only the best players at their respective universities.

After graduating he opened his own Law firm and successfully run it for two years. In 1909, he joined as a chief clerk a commission that was reviewing the Missouri statutes. He lated was appointed as the chief clerk of the Supreme court of Missouri. He sadly died in 1933 at only 48 years old.
