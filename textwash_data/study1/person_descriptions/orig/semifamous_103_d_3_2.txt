William P. Bettendorf was a German-American man who was born in Mendota, Illinois USA on 1st July 1857. He died on 3rd June 1910 aged 52 in Iowa USA. Bettendorf was an inventor and is most famous for inventing the power lift sulky plow, the Bettendorf metal wheel, and the one-piece railroad truck frame. He and his younger brother founded the Bettendorf axel company. William was married twice to Mary Wortman from 1879 to 1901 and Elizabeth Staby from 1908 to 1910. He had two children called Etta and Henry. The city of Bettendorf in Iowa is named after William and his brother Joseph. His final resting place is the Oakdale memorial gardens in Davenport, Iowa USA.
