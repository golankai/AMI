Dr. Brantley Ratke is a museum education officer for the world famous museum in Uganda. Dr. Ratke had a difficult start to life and grew up in the local orphanage after having lost both parents due to them sadly passing away when he was 2 years old. It was here though, that would shape the future of Dr. Brantley Ratke when he used his first colouring sets. The orphanage consisted of a playroom and one of the few toys he could use was a colouring sets and paint. Once he started painting, he couldn’t stop, painting hundreds of pictures every week. After studying art in school, he went on to complete his PHD in Oil Paintings where he was also able to work alongside Uganda’s museum as an education officer.
