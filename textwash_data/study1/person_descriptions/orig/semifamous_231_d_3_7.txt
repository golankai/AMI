Charles Black was an American legal academic and lecturer who died in 2001. 
He was born in Texas, and studied at the University of Texas, before graduating from Yale in 1943 with an LLB (bachelor of laws).
Black was involved in several civil rights cases.
In 1975, Black was appointed professor of Law at Yale university in Conneticut where he remained for 31 years. 
His published works included 'Impeachment: A Handbook' which was the standard textbook for information on the impeachment process and was referenced extensively during investigations into the Watergate scandal.
In his personal life, Black was a published poet, having developed an interest in poetry in his 40's.
