Born in Key West, Florida, in 1891,  Irvin Brooks, also referred to as Chester, was an American baseball player in the Negro Leagues. Brooks, averaging around 5'11"", was a scrappy and rather determined individual.

Brooks was right-handed, in both batting and throwing. He played for the Brooklyn Royal Giants and was considered one of the best of his time, where he was eventually added to Homestead Grays, one of the most influential Negro league all-star team.

Brooks went on to play baseball for 15 years, where he eventually left. Living a rich life, Irvin Woodberry 'Chester' Brooks, died at the age of 74.
