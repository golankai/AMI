Enoch Lincoln, born 1788, was the sixth governor of Maine. He was the son of Levi Lincoln Sr. and was born in Worcester, Massachusetts, Lincoln was know to have attended Harvard College and graduated in Law in 1807. Lincoln was admitted to the bar in 1811 and practiced in the notorious town of Salem. Following this, Lincoln served as United States distinct attorney from 1815-1818 before moving the Maine district where he continued to study law. A Democratic-Republican, Lincoln was elected to the Fifteenth Congress following the resignation of his predecessor Albion K. Parris in 1818. Lincoln held his office through several re-electios until his resignation in 1826. He then served as governor of Maine from 1827 until his his death in October 1829. He was succeeded by Nathan Cutler
