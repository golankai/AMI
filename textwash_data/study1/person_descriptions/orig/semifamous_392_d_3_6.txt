Malcolm Dunstan was a English cricket player who player between 1969 and 1989.
He was born in Redruth, Cornwall in 1950. His father was a fisherman who had served in the Royal Navy during WW2 and had been involved in the D-Day landings. His mother was a midwife. He had 3 elder brothers and a younger sister.
It school he excelled at sports, particularly cricket and football. He was expected to follow in his fathers footsteps, but at the age of 14 started playing for the 2nd eleven of the Cornwall minor counties team, and never looked back.
He debuted for the first eleven in 1969, and went on to represent them until 1989.
