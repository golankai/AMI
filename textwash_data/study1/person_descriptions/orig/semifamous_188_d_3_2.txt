William P. Bettendorf was born in 1857 and died in 1910. He was from Mendota, Illinois and was an inventor of various mechanical and truck parts and had 94 patents by the time he was 53. In his younger years he worked in a hardware store then got an apprenticeship at the Peru Plough Company. He married Mary Wortman in 1879 and they had two children, Etta and Henry. After Mary's death, he remarried, this time Elizabeth Staby, a widow. He also founded the Bettendorf Axle Company with his brother Joseph. It was very successful and was rapidly expanding when he died. The city of Bettendorf in Iowa was named after the two brothers.
