Born in 1976, Benedict Cumberbatch is a British actor who became a household name playing the titular character in BBC's ""Sherlock"" in 2010. He has starred prominently in blockbusters like ""Star Trek: Into Darkness"", ""War Horse"", and as the voice of Smaug the dragon in ""The Hobbit"" trilogy. He is currently most recognised for his role as Doctor Strange in the films that make up the Marvel Cinematic Universe. With a Masters in Classical Acting from the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art, his education has served him well in television, film, and on stage - winning him critically acclaimed performances, and the Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actor for ""Frankenstein"" in 2011. He is married with two children, is an ambassador for The Prince's Trust, and supports LGBT+ rights.
