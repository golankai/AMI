Enoch Lincoln, born December 28th, 1788, died October 8th, 1829, was a U.S. Representative from Massachusetts and Maine. He was born in Worcester, Massachusets and studied at Harvard college in 1807. He studied law there, ending up taking it on in Salem as a District attorney. (1811.) He also moved to Paris, continuing his profession.In in 1818, November, Lincoln was elected as a Democratic-Republican, representing the district of Maine. He served from November 4th, 1818, to March 3rd, 1821 as he was reelected. He took up this due to the resignation of Albion K. Parris.
Then 1819 he was elected as an American Antiquarian Society.
