William P. Bettendorf was one of 4 brothers whose passion was to invent, which he shared his passion with on eof his brother, Joseph W. Bettendorf. When William P. Bettendorf passed away at the early age of 52, he patented 94 inventions.  One of his most famous invention was the power lift sulky plow, which he invented while he started working as a machinist's apprentice at the Peru Plow Company in 1874. This invention allowed farmers to remotely lift the plow while they are seatw sin their horse drawn plough. Before the invention the farmer would had to get off the plough and manually lift the plough out of the ground, get back on the plough to turn around to start a new furrow and lift it back down into the earth.
