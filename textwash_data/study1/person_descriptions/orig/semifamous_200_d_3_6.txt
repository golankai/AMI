Malcom Dunstan is a 69 year old ex cricket player from Cornwall. During his early life he played cricket for Cornwall in the Minor Counties Championship. After a year, he moved onto playing cricket professionally, competing in the 1970 Gillette Cup, where his team was knocked out in the first round. During his first 12 matches, Dunstan achieved a batting average of 16.64. Dunstan played for Cornwall until 1971, where he was scouted for the Gloucestershire team. He remained there until 1974 where he subsequently played for the minor counties. Dunstan preferred to both bowl and bat with his right hand. Malcom Dunstan never married, preferring to dedicating his life to cricket.
