William P Bettendorf was a German-American inventor who was born in Iowa in 1857 and died at 52 years old. He had a round face, a double chin, a big nose and a moustache. He had very little hair due to his receding hairline. He is famous for inventing technological products such as the power lift sulky low. He married Mary Wortman when he was 22 and they raised two children, a boy and a girl. 22 years after their wedding day she died, and Bettendorf went on to remarry. Bettendorf built his own family's 22 acre estate. It featured large porches and a carriage house. This suggests Battendorf was a talented handyman.
