The late Charles Lund Black Jr. is a professor who was born in Austin, Texas on the 22nd of September 1915 and who sadly passed on May 5th 2001 in New york. He taught at the prestigious Yale law school for thirty-one years. In this time he achieved the position of Sterling Professor. He is most recognised for his work on the Brown vs Board of education case, which was a significant case that resulted in the abolishment of segregation in American schools (1954). He is famously known for teaching first lady Hilary Clinton at Yale law school.  He also wrote a collection of poetry books as a hobby, starting when he was 40 years old.
