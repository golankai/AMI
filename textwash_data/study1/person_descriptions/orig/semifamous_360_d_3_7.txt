Charles Black was an America scholar, and professor who taught law. He has also written a handbook analysing an impeachment law, following a notable court case. He was married to Babara Aronstein Black. 

He grew up in Austin, Texas, where he later attended the university in 1935. Following a degree in English, he progressed to Yale Law school, before working as a teacher, and later a professor of Law. 

In addition to his work as a professor, Black wrote many poems. He was particularly interested in race and civil rights, following a visit to a Louis Armstrong concert. 

Black died in New York City, aged 85.
