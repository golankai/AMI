Walter Chorn was a prominent lawyer and college football player. He first studied at Central College (later renamed Central Methodist University, or CMU), before studying at Vanderbilt University to gain at LLB. At Vanderbilt, Chorn was a guard and tackle for Vanderbilt's Commodores Football team, and was even once selected for the All-Southern (an all-stars team for those in southern states). He was also a fraternity member, more specifically Kappa Sigma. 
After Vanderbilt he opened his own law office in Fayette, Missouri (where Central College is located), practising for two years. He then switched to the public sector, working revising Missouri statutes, before serving as the chief clerk of the Supreme Court of Missouri.
