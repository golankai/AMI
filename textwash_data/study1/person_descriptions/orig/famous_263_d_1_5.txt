Sir Michael Philip Jagger, more professional known as Mick Jagger was born on 26 July 1943 in Dartford, Kent, England.
He is known worldwide for being the lead singer of an English rock group called The Rolling Stones. As well as his work as a singer, he also has notable credits as a songwriter, actor, and film producer. Outside of his band, he has worked with the likes of The Jackson 5, Tina Turner, David Bowie, Lenny Kravitz, U2 and many more. He has also been referenced in songs by other artists and has even had a funky song written about him by the band Maroon 5 titled ""Moves like Jagger"".
