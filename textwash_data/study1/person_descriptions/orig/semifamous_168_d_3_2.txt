William P. Bettendorf, born on 1st July 1857, is a renowned German-American inventor. After his death, in which his first wife Mary Wortman and their two children, preceded him, he was recognised for holding the patent for 94 inventions mostly of industrial use.
The Bettendorf Axel Company was founded alongside William's younger brother Joseph W. Bettendorf. 
Just as the company was rapidly expanding, William died before he moved into a palatial home. He died whilst receiving emergency surgery whilst living in a bungalow whilst his mansion was being constructed, The city of Bettendorf, Iowa is named after the two brothers.
