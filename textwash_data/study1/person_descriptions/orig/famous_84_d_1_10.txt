Sam Smith is a popular English singer and songwriter, most known for singing 'Writings on the Wall', a theme song for James Bond. They rose to fame in 2013 and has had an active music career since with hits in both the UK and the US. Their debut album, In the Lonely Hour, which featured the hit singles ‘Money on my Mind’, ‘Lay me down’ and ‘Stay with Me’, won four awards at the 2014 Grammys, including ‘Best New Artist’, ‘Record of the Year’ and ‘Song of the Year’. In 2017 Sam Smith came out as gender-queer and following on in 2019 Smith came out as Non-Binary and stated a preference for they/them pronouns.
