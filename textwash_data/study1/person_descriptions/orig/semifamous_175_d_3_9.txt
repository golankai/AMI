Walter Knaus Chorn, was born in Howard County, Missouri on January 21st in 1885. He was a college football star, playing Guard and Tackle for Vanderbilt university, Nashville, TN, where he won the All-Southern championship in 1906. After graduation he opened a law office in Fayette, Missouri, where he practised for two years. In 1909 he was awarded the position of chief clerk of the commission responsible for revising Missouri's statues. This was followed by a position in the state auditor's office in 1910 and 1911. What had started out a s a promising legal career was rewarded in 1913, when he became the chief clerk of the supreme court of Missouri. He died on February 26, 1933 in Kansas City, Missouri, aged just 48.
