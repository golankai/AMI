Charles Black (full name Charles Lund Black Jr.) was born in Austin Texas in September 1915. He died at the age of 85 in 2001. Charles was a professor of law at the Columbia University Law School. He is well known for writing ""Impeachment: A Handbook"" and for being involved in the 'Brown v. Board of Education Supreme Court' case. In his personal life, Charles Black wrote poetry publishing ""Telescopes and Islands"", ""Owls Bay in Babylon"" and ""The Walking Passenger"". He came to poetry late in life, starting to write at 40. Black had an interest in race and civil rights and held an annual 'Armstrong Evening' at Yale in honour of Louis Armstrong.
