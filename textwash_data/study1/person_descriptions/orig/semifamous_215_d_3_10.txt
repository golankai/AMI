Irvin Brooks was an American baseball player. A black man, he was born in Florida in 1891. He is believed to have West Indian ancestry and has been erroneously described as Bahamian in several history texts. Brooks spent his career with one baseball team, the Brooklyn Royal Giants, based in New York. He played several positions and was noted as a hitter. He was righthanded. He was nicknamed Chester. He lived and played ball in New York. As it was the early 20th century, and he was a black man, and Brooks had to play in leagues for non-white players called the Negro Leagues. He died at the age of 74 in 1966.
