Enoch Lincoln was born in Worcester, Massachusetts in 1788 and was educated at Harvard College studying law where he graduated in 1807. From 1811 he was admitted to the bar (gave permission by the court to begin his career as a lawyer) and started practicing as a district attorney in Salem from 1815-18. From here he moved to Paris, Massachusetts where he carried on his profession in 1819. 
In 1818 Enoch Lincoln stood for election for the Democratic-Republican Party in the Maine district where he served from 1818 until 1821.
In 1819 Lincoln was also elected a member of the American Antiquarian Society, which is a learned society and national research library. 
Enoch Lincoln died in Augusta, Maine in October of 1829
