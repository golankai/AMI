Enoch Lincoln was a U.S. representative from Maine. Born in 1788, he attended Harvard Collage in 1807, studying law, and began his professional law occupation in 1811. From 1815 he served as the Direct Attorney for the federal U.S. Government, until 1819 when he returned to practicing law. In 1818 Lincoln was voted the Democratic-Republican representative for Maine, serving the seventeenth, eighteenth and nineteenth congress. He continued in and was re-elected into this role until his resignation in 1826. He then served as Governor of Maine from 1827 until his death in 1829, he had won three terms in this position with over 90% of the vote. The town of Lincoln, Maine is named after him. He was also distantly related to U.S. president Abraham Lincoln.
