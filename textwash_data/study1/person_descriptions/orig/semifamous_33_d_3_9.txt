Walter K. Chorn was an American man who was born in 1885 in Missouri. He had dark hair and weighed 170 lb. During his adult life, Walter had a varied and rich career ranging from sports to law. He played college American football under coach Dan McGuin's Vanderbilt Commodores team while attending the Vanderbilt University in Nashville, Tennessee. During his time at university Walter was also a member of the fraternity Kappa Sigma. After graduating, he put his college football career to the side and went into the world of law. Initially he had a law office of his own but in 1909 he went on to become a chief clerk. His law career continue to prosper and in 1913 he was named chief clerk of the supreme court of Missouri.
