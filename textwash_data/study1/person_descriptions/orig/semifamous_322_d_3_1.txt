Enoch Lincoln was born on December 28,  1788, and died on October 8, 1829. He was the governor of Maine from 1827 until his death, in 1829. 
He started studying law at Harvard College and graduated in 1807. 
His early career began in 1811 when he was admitted to the bar and started practising his profession in Salem. He moved to Paris, France, after just 7 years and continued pursuing his career in the Law domain. 
Enoch was also a member of the American Antiquarian Society, in 1819. Apart from that, he was also elected as a Democratic-Republican, representing the Maine district. He was reelected in 1821, March 3.
