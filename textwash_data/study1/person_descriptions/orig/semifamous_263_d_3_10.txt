Irvin Woodberry ""Chester"" Brooks was born on June 5, 1891 and died on February 4, 1966. Although it has been written in several books that his country of birth was the Bahamas and his name was Chester Brooks, he was known as Irvin Brooks and was born in Key West, Florida. Irvin was most known as an African American baseball player who gained success playing with the Brooklyn Royal Giants in the Negro leagues. He remained with the Brooklyn Royal Giants throughout his entire career where he played pitcher, infielder, and outfielder from 1918 to 1933. He was named as the favourite Negro league baseball player of all time by the influential owner of the Homestead Grays, Cumberland Posey.
