Benedict Cumberbatch is a British actor most famous for his role as Sherlock Holmes in the TV series Sherlock and for playing Alan Turning in The Imitation Games. 

A graduate on Manchester University, Cumberbatch continued his training at the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Arts. He originally began his career as a stage actor working on the West End and preforming in a range of Royal National Theatre performances. 

Alongside his work as a stage and screen actor, Cumberbatch, 44, is an outspoken advocate for issues surrounding equality. In 2020 he announced that he would no longer be accepting roles where is female co-star would not be paid the same.
