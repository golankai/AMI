Malcolm Dunstan is a former English Cricketer who prolifically represented Cornwall throughout his career until he retired at age 39 in 1989. Although ambidextrous he played professionally as a right-handed batsman, only demonstrating his ambidexterity for Comic Relief in 1992, when challenged by Lenny Henry. He was born in Redruth in Cornwall to Mary and Arthur Dunstan, and is the middle child. His older brother is named Reece and his younger brother is the famous music composer Dewey Dunstan. Malcolm excelled at Chemistry in school but decided to not pursue this at degree level as his cricketing career was taking off
