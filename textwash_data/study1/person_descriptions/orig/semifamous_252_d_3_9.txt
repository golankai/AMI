Walter Knaus Chorn was born in Howard County in the state of Missouri in the United States of America towards the end of the 19th century. 

Walter received a full education attending college in Fayette and also Vanderbilt University in Nashville.

During his university years Walter played American Football and was a versatile member of the university's Commodore's team playing in either the Guard or Tackle position.

Walter graduated from Vanderbilt and went into the legal profession. Walter opened a Law Office in Fayette where he practiced for 2 years during which time he was selected to be the chief clerk of the commission that was responsible for making revisions to Missouri law. Further promotions followed in the ensuing years.

No record exists of Walter getting married or having children and details about his personal life are extremely difficult to get hold of.
