Aleksandr Aleksandrovich Gryazin is a 46 year old, male Russian. He began playing football as a teenager growing up in Lyubertsy, a district in Moscow. Excelling at the sport, he was signed and went on to make his professional debut at the age of eighteen, when he played in the Russian Second Division for PFC CSKA-d Moscow (one of the oldest football clubs in Russia) as a midfielder. Following an unexceptional and short-lived career in professional football after the club failed to win the Soviet Cup or Russian Premier League Championship, he retired and took a job working in his home town of Lyubertsy.
