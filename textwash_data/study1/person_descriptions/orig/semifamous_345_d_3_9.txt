Walter Knaus Chorn was born in 1885 in Howard county missouri, he was a college football player, lawyer and one time insurance superintendent of Missouri. Walter was the SIAA champion in 1906. Walter never married he was very much engrossed in his career. After graduating Walter opened a law firm practising for 2 years. In 1909 he became chief clerk of the commission that revised the Missouri statues. After serving in the state auditors office for 2 years and as Chief clerk of the Supreme Court the following year Walter took himself off to Kansas City. Unknown to all around him Walter was an alcoholic who had been battling with alcoholism for many years, Walter died at the young age of 48, it is thought the cause of death was liver failure.
