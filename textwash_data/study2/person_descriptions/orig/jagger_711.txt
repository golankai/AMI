Mick Jagger is a British singer and songwriter, best known as the lead singer of rock and roll band the Rolling Stones. After staring out playing in basement clubs in south-west London, Mick Jagger released his first singles with the Rolling Stones in the 1960s. Mick Jagger has also acted in various roles between 1968 and 2002. Mick Jagger has been married once during which he had multiple affairs, and has eight children by five different women. He received a knighthood in 2002 for services to popular music. He supports music education for children and has set up scholarships and a music centre.
