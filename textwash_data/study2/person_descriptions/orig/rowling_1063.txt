Joanne Rowling more commonly known as J.K. Rowling is a British author, philanthropist and writer and is best known for writing the Harry Potter series which was also adapted into a series of films. Born on 31st of July 1965 in Yate, Gloucestershire, Rowling had an unhappy childhood due to complications with their families health such as her mother's multiple sclerosis diagnosis and poor relationship with her father.
She attended some Michaels primary school where is it believed that her headmaster Alfred Dunn was the inspiration for the character of Albus Dumbledore in the Harry Potter series.
