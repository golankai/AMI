Mick Jagger is the lead singer of the rolling stones. He started the band with Keith Richards. He was born in 1946 on Dartford, Kent. One of his most famous hours was 'I can't get no satisfaction' Mick Jagger had Eight children. He became a father again at the age of 76 with his 29 year old girlfriend. In 1989 the rolling stones were inducted into the rock and roll hall of fame. Outside of the band Mick Jagger branched out into acting. In 1970 he played the title character Ned Kelly and a film called performance about a reclusive rock and roll star, neither films were very well received.
