Sam Smith (they/them) was born in London in May 1992 and began singing from a very young age. They first hit the charts as the featured singer on the 2012 hit single from Disclosure  ""Latch,"" and released their first single, ""Lay Me Down,"" following this success. In 2014, Smith released his debut album, In the Lonely Hour. It contained the top hit ""Stay With Me,"" a radio favourite for many months. For their work on In the Lonely Hour, Smith was awarded four Grammy awards,  including Song of the Year and Record of the Year, an excellent achievement from a debut.  In 2019, Smith announced that they prefer to use non-binary  pronouns, they/them.
