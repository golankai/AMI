Hugh Grant is a British actor who was born in 1960.  His first film was the 1982 movie 'Privileged', but he went on to much more prominent roles, notably Ken Russell's ""Lair of The White Worm"", ""Notting Hill"" ""Four Weddings and A Funeral"".  For a while, he was synonymous with a certain type of British male actors, who specialised in romantic leads especially for the American market.  He famously dated Elizabeth Hurley, who made newspaper headlines wearing ""that dress"", which was a black cut-out number, with oversized gold safety pins.  Their relationship ended when Hugh was apprehended soliciting sex from a prostitute named Divine Brown in the USA.  His mugshot was featured worldwide.
