Born in July 1989, Daniel Radcliffe is an English actor, best known for his role as 'Harry Potter' in the film franchise. His first TV appearance was in an adaptation of 'David Copperfield' for the BBC in 1999, when Radcliffe was aged 10. Following the Harry Potter series, which commenced in 2001 with 'Harry Potter and the Philsopher's Stone', and concluded in 2011 with 'Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (Part Two)', he has appeared in a number of stage productions and independently released films. He has supported both the UK Liberal Democrat and Labour parties. Radcliffe has expressed pride in his Jewish heritage, and since 2012, has been in a relationship with the actress Erin Darke.
